Hispaniola

Caribbean Paradise

Hispaniola is the 22nd-largest island in the world,located in the Caribbean island group,the Greater Antiles.It is the second largest island inthe Caribbean after Cuba,and the tenth most populous island in the world.

Hispaniola isthe first permanent European settlement in the Americas,founded by Christopher Columbus in his voyagess in 1492 and 1493.

Geography

HIspaniola is the second largest island of the West Indies,with an area of about 76,129km2.The name given to the island was Columbus.Hispaniola means "small Spain".The Tainos called the island "Quisqueya",Mother Earth.

From a geological point of view,the island of Hispaniola is part of Two branches of theNorth American cordillera system and is about 660km long,250km wide and extendswest-easterly.In the west,the Island of Hispaniola is seperated by the aprroximately 90km wide Winward Passage from Cuba and in the east by the MOna Paasage of Puerto Rico,which is up to 110km wide.

On the island of Hispaniola live five large mountain chains.the highest mountains of the Caribbean are in the Dominican Republic.The highest mountain is the Pico Duerte with 3,097 metres above sea level.Also the deepest point of the Caribbean lies on Hispaniola:the salt lake Enriquillo in the southwest of the Dominican Republic.

Hispaniola offers four vegetation zones and five climatic zones.More than 5,000 plant species,many of them endemic,make the island of paradise for nature lovers.The animal world is also convincing,although it is not possible to affect large animals except for a few species.For this,the bird world is represented even more arsenically.

History

The island of Hispaniola (La Isla Espanola) was the first colony in the New World settled by Spain.

As such,it served as the logistical base for the conquest of most of the Western Hemisphere,Christopher Columbus first sighted the island in 1492 toward the end of his first voyage to "the Indies".

For Two centuries the Spanish ruled the island until western Hispaniola(present-day Haiti) came under French control in 1697.The east still remained under Spanish control.This created a division tat still exists today,with the Dominican Republic and Haiti sharing the island.

Finally,and after decades of unstable political situations,on 27 February 1844,the territory of Santo Domingo recovered its sovereignity and declared as independence as the Dominican Republic.
